   <h3 class="edTag">One financial step forward – and two steps back</h3>   <p>Over the long-term, experts say the financial aid like enhanced food stamps will help improve lives.</p>   <p>But with the pandemic still lingering as the omicron variant continues to spread across the country, families find that the help is often still not enough.</p>   <p>"Long term we will see the policy shifts will benefit families. But where we are right now we can't say that because we still are in the pandemic," said Aisha Nyandoro, CEO of Springboard To Opportunities, a charity helping families living in Jackson's affordable housing. </p>   <aside id="ad-secondary-wrap" aria-label="advertisement">

            Mandel Ngan/AFP via Getty Images
            
        </span>
    </div>
</div>
   </div>
   <h3 class="edTag">Child Tax Credit stuck in limbo</h3>   <p>Another program that received a lot of praise from poverty advocates is the Child Tax Credit, a Biden administration initiative that was <a href="https://www.npr.org/2021/05/17/997698543/new-monthly-child-tax-checks-will-hit-bank-accounts-july-15-what-you-need-to-kno">approved</a> by Congress as part of the American Rescue Plan in March.</p>   <p>In July, the program began sending monthly payments to families based on the number of children and their age — $300 a month for each child under six and $250 a month for every other child under 18. Parents will also get an additional lump-sum payment when they file their taxes this year. </p>   <p>The Center on Poverty & Social Policy at Columbia University credits the Child Tax Credit as a major reason why the U.S poverty rate fell 2 percentage points  from June to July, or right after the first payment started going out.</p>   <p>Nyandoro, the CEO of the charity Springboard to Opportunity, recalls the excitement of a mother who was due to receive the Child Tax Credit.</p>   <p>"She talked about how this was the first time ... she would be able to take her kids to the store to pick out their backpacks, because they were getting ready for back to school time," Nyandoro recalled. </p>   <div id="res1073235705" class="bucketwrap internallink insettwocolumn inset2col ">
            <div class="bucket img">
                  <a id="featuredStackSquareImage1069143123" href="https://www.npr.org/2021/12/30/1069143123/expanded-child-tax-credit-expires-friday-congress"  data-metrics='{"category":"Story to Story","action":"Click Internal Link","label":"https:\/\/www.npr.org\/2021\/12\/30\/1069143123\/expanded-child-tax-credit-expires-friday-congress"}' ><picture><source srcset="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100-c15.webp" data-original="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100.webp" class="img lazyOnLoad"  type="image/webp" /><source srcset="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100-c15.jpg" data-original="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100.jpg" class="img lazyOnLoad"  type="image/jpeg" /><img src="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100-c15.jpg" data-original="https://media.npr.org/assets/img/2021/12/30/ap21349765768717_sq-fd8f5f51cc46b0d10f2a091c6efed9f6b2125e92-s100.jpg" class="img lazyOnLoad"  alt="The expanded child tax credit expires Friday after Congress failed to renew it" loading="lazy" /></picture></a>         <div class="bucketblock">

   </div>

   <p>But the program expired at the end of the year, and it has not  been renewed. Democrats want to bring it back this year, but <a href="https://www.npr.org/2021/12/30/1069143123/expanded-child-tax-credit-expires-friday-congress">Senator Joe Manchin opposes </a>the current version of the program and wants to add work requirements and an income cap in order to gain his support.</p>   <p>Laura Sifuentes from Rosdale, Miss.,<strong> </strong>says<strong> </strong>the Child Tax Credit provided her with critical help after the pandemic forced her to leave her job with a local Head Start program when schools went virtual and she had to take care of her kids.  </p>   <p>The monthly payments allowed her pay for things like the National Honor Society registration fee for her daughter and new clothes for her growing boys.</p>   <p>"It's a big help because I'm able to do more for my children," she said. </p>   <p>But even with the payment, she still had to watch what she spent. And with the payment now gone, she again needs to be even more careful as she looks for a job, one that can hopefully match her previous $12-per-hour job.</p>   <p>"The CTC has been a blessing," Sifuentes said recalling how the Child Tax Credit helped her family. "But now I have to be more smart about the way I shop. "</p>
